# Agentic AI

AI with **agency** — the ability to do things autonomously, with little or much supervision, "just like people." Per [[jensen-huang|Jensen Huang]] in [[jensen-huang-cna-interview]].

## The agentic pipeline (as described)
1. **Context / request** — what's the environment and what is being asked ("you are an excellent software engineer; access these files and write the software with me").
2. **Understand** the context.
3. **Plan** the work.
4. **Use tools** — browser, word editor, a C compiler, a Python editor, even a CUDA program.
5. **Perform → evaluate → re-evaluate → replan → act again**, iterating until the job is done.

## Two embodiments
- **Digital** — "AI agents."
- **Physical** — the same agent running inside a body: "a robot is essentially a physical AI agent." Huang expects both to be "highly generalized."

## Connections
- Sits at the **models/applications** layers of the [[ai-five-layer-cake]].
- The tool-use + iterate loop is why he argues a [[ai-and-jobs|job is a "basket of tasks"]] that agents automate piecewise rather than wholesale.
