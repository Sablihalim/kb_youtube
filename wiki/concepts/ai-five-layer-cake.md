# AI as a five-layer cake

[[jensen-huang|Jensen Huang]]'s framing that AI should be understood as a whole **industry stack**, not "just a model." Introduced in [[jensen-huang-cna-interview]].

## The five layers (bottom → top)
1. **Energy** — AI intelligence is *generated in real time*, so like a human needing calories, the industry needs power at the base. Regions limited by energy "will be left behind."
2. **Chips** — where [[nvidia|Nvidia]] and much of [[tsmc|Taiwan]]'s ecosystem sit (AI chips, DRAM, packaging, power regulators, cooling, MLCCs).
3. **Infrastructure** — land, power, data centers, and the cloud/software that turns chips into a service.
4. **Models** — what most people picture as "AI" ([[chatgpt|ChatGPT]], [[claude-code|Claude Code]]). Crucially **not just human language** — also proteins, chemicals, images/video, and **3D geometry** (vital for manufacturing).
5. **Applications** — the societal impact layer: information workers, software/chip engineers, plant managers, self-driving, robotics, healthcare. "AI is reinventing every industry."

## Why it matters
- Reframes "is AI overhyped?" — the value isn't only at the model layer; the build-out drives jobs and investment across **all** layers (see [[ai-and-jobs]]).
- Underlies his [[ai-us-china-competition|US–China argument]]: Nvidia competes in *one* of the five layers, but its presence "supports the expansion of the other layers."
