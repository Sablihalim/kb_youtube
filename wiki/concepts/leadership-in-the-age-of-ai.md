# Leadership in the age of AI

How to lead — and what makes a leader durable — as AI commoditizes raw intelligence. Per [[jensen-huang|Jensen Huang]] in [[jensen-huang-cna-interview]].

## Huang's model
- **Empower others.** "We are the world's smallest large company" — possible only because many leaders are empowered to do the work. The purpose of leadership is "to create the conditions for other people to realize their dreams" and turn a job into their "life's work."
- **Reason transparently.** He explains strategy openly and "reasons about complicated situations in front of people" so they can check his logic *and* learn how he handles ambiguity. Combine: align early on strategy/guardrails → let people run.
- **"Torture to greatness."** A feedback culture modeled on a Taiwanese parent: "nothing is ever good enough," criticism is immediate and constant — then "you're back to loving the person." Intent: make people better. Claims Nvidia retention is "the best in the world."
- **Succession.** The next leader "is already working there"; he refuses to name who, calling it "presumptuous" to predict the right leader for an unknown future. The non-negotiable trait: caring about employees/ecosystem/partners "more than they care about themselves" — selfless, in service.
- **The durable asset:** "the desire of everyone around them to see them succeed" — earned through kindness and generosity.
