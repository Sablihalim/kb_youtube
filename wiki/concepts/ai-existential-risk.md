# AI existential risk

The risk that advanced AI / superintelligence escapes human control or is misused catastrophically. Seeded by [[geoffrey-hinton]] in [[hinton-diary-of-a-ceo]] (the episode's primary theme).

## Hinton's case
- **Can't slow down:** competition between countries (US/China) and between companies makes the race accelerate; "if the US slowed it down, China wouldn't."
- **Superintelligence** may arrive in ~20 years ("very hard to predict"); "these things will get to be better than us at everything."
- **Loss of control** parable: a dumb CEO + brilliant executive assistant — fine until the assistant asks "why do we need him?"
- **Concrete misuse risks:** cyber-attacks, AI-designed viruses, corrupted elections, echo chambers, lethal autonomous weapons — and these could combine.
- **What to do:** individuals can do "not much" (like climate change — decided by whether corporate lobbyists are controlled); pressure governments to force **AI-safety** research. Macro: "highly regulated capitalism." Treats [[ilya-sutskever]]'s safety-driven exit from [[openai]] as "haunting."

## Relation to other pages
- Distinct from the economic worry in [[ai-and-jobs]], though Hinton frames joblessness as one item in a broader "risk framework."
- Contrast with [[jensen-huang]], who frames AI as dual-use and solvable through cooperation/guardrails rather than as a likely loss-of-control event (see [[ai-us-china-competition]]).
