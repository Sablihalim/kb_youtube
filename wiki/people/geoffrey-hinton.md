# Geoffrey Hinton

"Godfather of AI" — pioneer of deep learning / neural networks (AlexNet lineage), 2018 Turing Award, 2024 Nobel Prize in Physics. Left Google in 2023 to speak freely about AI risk. Central guest of [[hinton-diary-of-a-ceo]].

## Positions & views
- **AI & jobs (pessimist):** "this time is different" — AI replaces *intelligence*, not just tasks; "for mundane intellectual labour, AI is just going to replace everybody," meaning far fewer workers. The pessimist pole of [[ai-and-jobs]], directly opposing [[jensen-huang]].
- **Inequality:** expects AI to widen the rich–poor gap; UBI is "a good start" but doesn't restore the dignity tied to work.
- **[[ai-existential-risk|Existential risk]]:** his main cause — fears loss of control to a superintelligence (~20 years out, hard to predict); says we can't slow the race because of US/China and corporate competition.
- **Prescription:** "highly regulated capitalism"; pressure governments to force AI-safety work.
- **Career hedge he gives:** "train to be a plumber" (manual trades are safer than knowledge work for now).

## Notes
- Remarkable family tree: descended from George Boole (Boolean algebra) and Mary Everest Boole; relative of George Everest (Mt. Everest) and Joan Hinton (Manhattan Project physicist who later moved to China).
