# Jensen Huang

Founder and CEO of [[nvidia|Nvidia]]; the central subject of [[jensen-huang-cna-interview]] (CNA, 2026).

## Positions & views (as they appear across sources)
- **AI is a new industry, not just a model** — articulates the [[ai-five-layer-cake]] framework.
- **Contrarian on AI job loss** — "you won't lose your job to AI, but to someone who learned AI"; predicts "more jobs in 5 years." Calls the layoff narrative "too lazy… irresponsible." See [[ai-and-jobs]].
- **Compete *and* cooperate with China** — China is "everybody's greatest rival," but two separate AI ecosystems would be "not wise." See [[ai-us-china-competition]].
- **Taiwan-centric view of the AI supply chain** — close ties to [[tsmc]] and its founder [[morris-chang]]; Nvidia's Grace Blackwell + Vera Rubin imply ~$1T of sales flowing largely to Taiwan.
- **Leadership** — see [[leadership-in-the-age-of-ai]]: empower others, reason transparently, "torture to greatness." When [[intelligence-as-a-commodity|intelligence is a commodity]], character and ambition are the edge.

## Biography & character (from the interview)
- **Origins:** born in Taiwan, lived in Thailand, emigrated to the US "with nothing." Came back to Taiwan (to see [[morris-chang]]) on his first return after leaving at age 5.
- **Parents:** father precise/technical ("perfect handwriting"); mother obsessed with detail ("can't let anything go"). Credits their sacrifice with instilling his character; "we're all the products of our parents."
- **Work ethic:** works seven days a week, "exhausted all the time." Has focused on Nvidia for **33 years**, "equally obsessed" daily. Twin drivers: fear of failure (people are counting on him) + a hopeful, "in-a-hurry" dreamer.
- **"I hope to die on the job — that would be a dream come true."** Both his children work at Nvidia; wife **Lori Huang** has been at every Nvidia conference and speech.
- Longest-tenured big-tech CEO; built Nvidia into a multi-hundred-billion-dollar company growing ~100%/yr ("the world's smallest large company").
- Joined President Trump's 2026 Beijing trip last-minute (boarded Air Force One in Alaska).
